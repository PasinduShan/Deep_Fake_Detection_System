Video Prediction - Multi Model

De-Sight's multimodal deepfake detection leverages three AI models — I1 (Image Prediction Model), V1 (Video Prediction Model), and A1 (Audio Prediction Model) — to ensure accurate and reliable deepfake identification. The workflow begins by extracting both video frames and audio from the uploaded video. First, the I1 model analyzes individual frames to detect image-level manipulations. Next, the V1 model processes the sequence of frames to capture temporal inconsistencies. Simultaneously, the A1 model examines the extracted audio, identifying any mismatches between speech patterns and facial movements. Finally, the system combines the predictions from all three models by calculating an average confidence score, providing a comprehensive real vs. fake assessment. This multimodal approach enhances detection accuracy by cross-verifying results across visual and audio data.

How De-Sight Multimodal Deepfake Detection Works

De-Sight uses a powerful multimodal AI system to detect deepfakes with high accuracy. It combines insights from images, video sequences, and audio to ensure reliable detection.

  • 🧠 Our Three AI Models:
    • I1 – Image Model
      Analyzes individual video frames for visual manipulation.
    • V1 – Video Model
      Examines frame sequences to detect unnatural motion or inconsistencies.
    • A1 – Audio Model
      Checks if the audio matches facial movements and speech patterns.
  • 🔄 Detection Workflow:
    • Upload Video – De-Sight extracts frames and audio.
    • Analyze Images – I1 checks for altered or fake visuals.
    • Analyze Video – V1 tracks frame-to-frame consistency.
    • Analyze Audio – A1 verifies speech and lip-sync accuracy.
    • Final Result – All results are combined into one clear confidence score:
      Real or Fake.
  • ✅ Why It Works:
    • By using three types of data (image, video, audio) together, De-Sight cross-verifies findings, improving accuracy and reducing false results.
